1. Employment Relationship

Politicians are public employees, hired through elections, paid by taxpayers, with fiduciary duties to serve the public interest.

2. Breach of Duty

When politicians serve special interests over public interests, they breach their employment duties and create measurable harm.

3. Financial Liability

Breach of fiduciary duty creates legal liability for damages‚Äîthis is established law for all other employees.

4. Equal Protection

The 14th Amendment requires equal treatment under law. Politicians cannot have different accountability standards than other public employees.

üèõÔ∏è The Legal Hypocrisy

Our legal system already recognizes these principles:

  • Corporate executives are liable for breach of fiduciary duty
  • Trustees must account for mismanagement
  • Agents owe damages when they serve themselves instead of principals
  • Public employees face consequences for misconduct

But politicians claim special immunity from these same laws! This violates equal protection and creates a two-tiered justice system.

üí∞ Quantifiable Damages to the Public

When politicians breach their duties, they create real financial harm:

  • Misappropriated taxpayer funds - Money spent on special interests instead of public good
  • Economic losses from corrupt policies - Laws that benefit donors at public expense
  • Opportunity costs - Lost benefits from serving special interests over public needs
  • Cleanup costs - Expense of reversing harmful decisions
  • Administrative waste - Resources spent on hypocritical and contradictory laws

These damages are measurable, documentable, and create legitimate debt to the American people.
